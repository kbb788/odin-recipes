Chocolate Toffee Saltines

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (227g) unsalted butter, or salted
  • 1 cup (220g) packed light brown sugar, or dark
  • 40 (1 sleeve) salted saltine crackers
  • 270g (1 package) milk chocolate chips, or semi-sweet
  • Nuts or additional toppings
  • Candy or sprinkles (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Line a large baking sheet with heavy-duty aluminum foil.
  3. If using regular foil, spray it lightly with non-stick cooking spray for easy removal.
  4. Arrange 40 saltine crackers in a single layer on the foil.
  5. Fold up the edges of the foil to form a box around the crackers. Set aside.
  6. In a small saucepan, add 1 cup butter and 1 cup packed brown sugar.
  7. Place the saucepan over medium-high heat and melt the mixture, stirring occasionally.
  8. Bring the mixture to a rolling boil.
  9. Once boiling, reduce the heat to medium.
  10. Continue boiling for exactly 5 minutes (set a timer), stirring occasionally.
  11. Immediately pour the hot toffee mixture over the crackers.
  12. Use a spatula to spread the toffee evenly over all the crackers.
  13. Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven.
  14. Bake for 5 minutes, or until the toffee is bubbling.
  15. Turn off the oven and remove the baking sheet.
  16. Immediately sprinkle 270g (1 package) chocolate chips over the hot toffee.
  17. Let the chocolate chips sit for 1–2 minutes, until they begin to look shiny.
  18. Once softened, use a spatula to spread the chocolate evenly over the toffee.
  19. If the chocolate isn’t soft enough to spread after 4 minutes, return the tray to the oven for 1 minute to help it melt.
  20. Sprinkle optional toppings over the melted chocolate (e.g., chopped nuts, candy pieces, or sprinkles).
  21. Transfer the baking sheet to the refrigerator.
  22. Chill for about 2 hours, or until the toffee and chocolate are completely hardened.
  23. Once chilled, peel the foil away from the bottom.
  24. Break the cracker candy into pieces.
  25. Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 1 week.
  26. You can also freeze the candy for up to 3 months.
  27. Tip: A 10×15″ jelly roll pan works best. If using a larger pan (like 11×17″), fold foil edges up to prevent the toffee from spilling out.
  28. Tip: Heavy-duty foil peels away easily. If using regular foil, spray it. Parchment paper also works and doesn’t require greasing.
  29. Tip: Ensure the toffee mixture is boiling hard during the 5-minute cook — this helps create a caramel-like texture.
  30. Tip: The toffee should bubble during baking to ensure it absorbs into the crackers properly.
  31. Tip: If chocolate chips don’t melt easily, consider using mini chips or pre-melting chocolate in the microwave before spreading.
